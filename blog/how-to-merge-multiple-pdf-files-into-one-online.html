Tips for a Perfect Merged PDF

📋 Plan your order before uploading

Think about how the final document should read — cover page first, then content, then appendices. Upload files in that exact order. The merged PDF preserves the sequence perfectly.

📦 Compress after merging for large files

If your merged PDF turns out larger than expected (which can happen when combining image-heavy files), run it through the PDF Compressor to reduce the size before sending. You can often cut the merged file down by 50–70% with no visible quality loss.

🔒 Safe for confidential documents

Unlike most online mergers that upload your files to cloud servers, AIFreePDF processes everything locally in your browser. Your documents are never transmitted anywhere — making it completely safe for financial records, legal contracts, personal IDs, and medical files.

📱 Works on mobile

Frequently Asked Questions

How many PDF files can I merge at once?

There's no hard limit. You can merge as many files as you need. Very large batches (50+ files or 500+ pages total) may take a few extra seconds depending on your device's processing speed.

Will the page layout and formatting be preserved?

Yes — the merge tool combines your PDFs exactly as they are. Fonts, images, page sizes, and layouts are fully preserved. There is no re-rendering or quality loss.

Can I merge PDFs with different page sizes?

Yes. If one PDF is A4 and another is US Letter, the merged document will contain both — each page retains its original dimensions. There's no forced resizing.

Is it safe to merge confidential PDFs online?

With AIFreePDF, yes — completely. All processing runs in your browser. Your files never get uploaded to any server. No one has access to your documents, including us.

Can I merge PDFs on my phone?

Yes. Open AIFreePDF in Chrome (Android) or Safari (iPhone), select your files from your files app or downloads, and download the merged PDF to your device. No app installation needed.

What if I need to reorder pages after merging?

Use the Reorder Pages tool — drag and drop page thumbnails to rearrange them into the exact sequence you need, then save.
